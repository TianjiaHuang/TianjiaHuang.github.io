Howdy! My name is Tianjia Huang and I am a fourth-year Ph.D student of petroleum engineering at Texas A&M University (Advisor: Prof. George Moridis and Prof. Thomas Blasingame).

My research focuses on numerical simulation of subsurface energy, such as geothermal, hydrogen storage, CCUS, nuclear waste disposal, and particle transport in porous media. I am also interested in developing high-performance reservoir simulator, using MPI, OpenMP, and CUDA.

FORTRAN and MATLAB are two programming languages I use most often. I currently mainly use TOUGH software, which is developed in Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory, inclduing TOUGH+RealGasBrine (Parallelized by Dr. Moridis and me), TOUGH+Particle Transport (Developed and parallelized by me), TOUGH+Hydrate, and TOUGH+FLAC3D.
