Research Interests


  1. Numerical modeling of coupled thermal-hydraulic-mechanical (THM) dynamics in subsurface environment
    • Underground hydrogen storage in aquifers/depleted hydrocarbon reservoirs
    • Geologic carbon storage in saline formations/oil and natural gas reservoirs
    • Geologic disposal of spent nuclear fuel
    • Numerical modeling of particle transport within porous media
      • Sand production model in gas hydrate-bearing sediments
      • Modeling of particulate flow using Discrete Element Method (DEM)
      • High performance computing for petroleum science
        • Parallelization of TOUGH+ using MPI.
        • Parallel performance analysis using tools like TAU performance system
        • Code development, including TOUGH2, TOUGH+, and TOUGH+FLAC3D.

        I am proficient in the Fortran programming language, which I actively apply in the development of the TOUGH software suite from Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. Within this domain, I have been extensively involved in the enhancement and parallelization of various TOUGH modules. I have played a significant role in parallelizing TOUGH+RealGasBrine. I have also independently developed and parallelized the separate Particle Transport code (over 10,000 lines).
