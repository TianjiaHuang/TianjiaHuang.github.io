Research Interests

I am proficient in the Fortran/Matlab programming language, which I actively apply in the development of the TOUGH software suite from Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. Within this domain, I have been extensively involved in the enhancement and parallelization of various TOUGH modules.

  1. Numerical modeling of coupled thermal-hydraulic-mechanical (THM) dynamics in subsurface environment
    • Underground hydrogen storage in aquifers/depleted hydrocarbon reservoirs
    • Geologic carbon storage in saline formations/oil and natural gas reservoirs
    • Geologic disposal of spent nuclear fuel
  2. Numerical modeling of particle transport within porous media
    • Sand production model in gas hydrate-bearing sediments
    • Modeling of particulate flow using Discrete Element Method (DEM)
  3. High performance computing for petroleum science
    • Parallelization of TOUGH+ using MPI.
    • Parallel performance analysis using tools like TAU performance system
    • Code development and numerical simulation, including TOUGH2, TOUGH+, and TOUGH+FLAC3D
