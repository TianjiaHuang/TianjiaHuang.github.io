--- layout: page title: Research permalink: /research/ --- Underground Hydrogen Storage

Underground hydrogen storage emerges as a promising solution to counterbalance the discord between fluctuating renewable energy generation and the constant demand for energy, especially in the context of transitioning from fossil fuels to renewable sources. Utilizing subterranean structures like aquifers and depleted gas reservoirs offers a scalable, high-pressure, secure, cost-efficient, and eco-friendly means of hydrogen storage. Using TOUGH+RealGasBrine, I investigate several aspects regarding the hydrogen storage performance, including leakage into caprock, effect of cushion gas (N2, CO2, CH4), effect of reservoir sites and so on.


Nuclear Waste Disposal1

Deposal of high level nuclear wastes is developing and implementing safe geological repositories for all nuclear waste arising from the use of nuclear energy and from medicine, industry and research. Coupled thermal-hydro-mechanical numerical modeling is conducted to study such problem. Using TOUGH+RealGasBrine and FLAC3D, I investigate the temperature, pressure, and stress response associated with geologic disposal of high-level radioactive nuclear waste over 10,000 years.
